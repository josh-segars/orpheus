LinkedIn Data · Step 1 of 2

Request Your Data Archive

LinkedIn can export a comprehensive archive of your professional data including your profile, work history, connections, skills, and recommendations. This file forms the primary data input for your diagnostic and takes up to 24 hours for LinkedIn to prepare.

Plan ahead

LinkedIn may take up to 24 hours to prepare your file. We recommend completing this step first so the rest of your Groundwork can happen while you wait.

1

Open Settings & Privacy

From any LinkedIn page, click your profile photo or the Me icon at the top of the screen. Select Settings & Privacy from the dropdown menu.

2

Navigate to Download your data

In the left sidebar, select Data privacy. Then choose Download your data from the options that appear.

3

Select the full archive

Choose Download larger data archive — the first option. This includes your connections, profile, recommendations, and the full history LinkedIn holds on your account.

Screenshot: Download my data — selection screen
4

Request the archive

Click Request archive. The page will update to confirm your request and display a timestamp. You will receive an email from LinkedIn when your file is ready.

Screenshot: Request pending confirmation screen

You can close this page while you wait. There is no need to leave it open.

5

Return and download when ready

Once you receive LinkedIn's email notification, return to the same Download your data page. You will see a Download archive button listing the files included. Click it to save the ZIP file to your computer.

Screenshot: Download archive — ready screen
6

Upload your file below

Upload the ZIP file as downloaded. There is no need to extract or modify its contents.
