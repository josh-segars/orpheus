Forward Brief

Your Path to a Stronger Signal

Based on your Signal Score diagnostic, these five priorities represent the clearest opportunities to close the gap between where your presence is and where your experience warrants. They are ordered by leverage — address them in sequence for compounding effect.

1
Grow Your Follower Base
Reach
Follower count is the most visible credibility signal to someone who encounters your profile for the first time. Your audience composition is excellent; you need more of them.
Connect with 10–15 new targeted professionals per week from your target industries. Follow up every post with direct engagement (reply to every comment within 24 hours) to signal to the algorithm that your content is worth amplifying. Set a milestone: 1,000 followers in 90 days.
2
Establish a Publishing Rhythm
Consistency
Algorithmic visibility on LinkedIn is almost entirely a function of consistent signal — not volume, not quality alone, but reliable presence. Your current sporadic pattern means weeks of invisibility.
Commit to a non-negotiable minimum of 3 posts per week, published Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. Use a content bank — write 2–3 weeks of posts at once to eliminate the weekly decision cost. Consistency at moderate quality outperforms quality at low frequency.
3
Request Recommendations Strategically
Presence
You have 3 recommendations received against 8 given — a visible imbalance. Recommendations are the only form of third-party validation visible on your profile, and they carry disproportionate weight with high-seniority visitors.
Identify 3–5 colleagues, clients, or supervisors from your most recent and most prestigious roles. Send a specific, personal request — tell them what you'd like them to highlight. Aim for 2 new recommendations in the next 30 days.
4
Publish Your First LinkedIn Article
Presence
Long-form articles index differently on LinkedIn and in search. They signal depth of thought leadership that posts alone cannot establish. Zero articles is a notable gap for a senior advisor.
Write one 800–1,200 word article on a topic at the intersection of your expertise and your audience's biggest current challenge. Focus on a specific question you are asked frequently. Publish it, then share it as a post with a short excerpt and a "read the full piece" call to action.
5
Sharpen Your Content Voice
Resonance
Your engagement rate is functional, but the gap between your average post and your top performers suggests untapped potential. The content that resonates most tends to be specific, personal, and stake-forward — not advisory or observational.
Review your 5 highest-performing posts for patterns. Before writing any post, answer: "What is the specific situation I am in, what do I actually think about it, and why would that matter to someone in my audience?" Posts that answer all three tend to outperform. Eliminate posts that are purely professional updates or content shares without a personal angle.
